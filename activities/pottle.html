Pottle


The 3 principle receptacles, shown here with 3 chairmen of Glee.
Left to right, The Tray (Mike Houston), The Pottle (Stuart Roberts) and The Cup (Valentin Mahrwald)1


Pottle is the Glee game of choice, at least it is when we're at the residential. The ideal location and equipment are to be found in St. Mary's Church, Cottonstones.

Pottle is a game for any number of people (although probably more than 4), involving a bouncy ball and a number of containers in which to (attempt to) catch it - the 'receptacles'. The ball is thrown around, people try to catch it, there is much running around2, and a great deal of fun is had by all.
The main three receptacles used (as seen in the photo) are the tray, the cup and most importantly, the pottle itself (so called because it is not quite a pot, and not quite a kettle). If there are enough people playing, further receptacles can be added, utilising whatever is lying around. In the past, a bin lid has served us well.

Ideally, a non-contact endeavour.3

The rules were, at one point, codified by Toby Wardman on the pottle website, which is sadly no more.

'Quiet Pottle' is a more controversial variant.
