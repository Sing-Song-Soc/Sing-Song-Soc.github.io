History

1989 - New Beginnings

Four members of the University of York Gilbert & Sullivan Society, led by a visiting American student, Erik Contzius, founded a barbershop quartet. The founding members were Erik Contzius (Baritone), Jeremy Lowe (Bass), Mark Broomfield (Tenor 1), and Christopher Brenner (Tenor 2).

At first, we shared many traditions (and members!) with G&S. Our original constitution was based on theirs, and for several years people who joined both societies paid reduced membership fees.

"Mid-Nineties" - Becoming Unique

Glee's unique identity began to emerge in the mid-Nineties, however, when for three years we became probably the world's only combined choir and Cephalopod Appreciation Society. (It's a long story.)1

1997 - Musical Time!

In association with DramaSoc, we produced a full-length musical comedy, written by us. As well as this, for seven years2 we toured local primary schools with a short musical, the last four of which were written by ourselves

1998 - Rebranding

Initially known as the "Glee Club" following American tradition, in 1998 we became York Glee Singers because most people didn't realise we were a choir.

2002 - The Glee CD

Glee records a full-length studio CD, more can be found on this here.

2010 - 20th Anniversary Celebration

Or if you must - Gleeunion 2010!

19th-20th June 2010

A few of us gathered to celebrate what was, more or less, 20th anniversary of the choir, sang some songs, had a lovely meal out and generally reminisced. Much fun was had by all.3

2019 - Rebranding, again

Goodbye York Glee Singers, hello Sing Song Soc. The change happened between the Spring and Summer concerts and was prompted by, once again, people not thinking we're a choir. (Thanks Glee (2009))4

2020 - The 30th AnniversaGLEE concert

The Sing Song Soc Spring 2020 concert took place in the University of York Central Hall, at 7.30pm, on Thursday 14th March.

The concert was a celebration of the choir's anniversary - 'The 30th AnniversaGLEE concert'! The programme was a selection of old favourites from previous concerts (obviously, skewing a bit more recent) and a few pieces to represent the decades the choir has been running.

2024 - Sing Song on BBC Radio 1

On the 26th of September 2024, Greg James came to Central Hall to hold a funeral for a duck. We sang a parody of Chappell Roan's HOT TO GO (L-O-N-G B-O-I) and See You Again by Wiz Khalifa, both live on BBC Radio 1. It is one of the silliest things I've ever had the pleasure of being part of.
